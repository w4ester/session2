The 6 Core Components

1. Name & Description

What it's called and what it does (public-facing)

2. Instructions

The "brain" - tells the GPT how to think and respond

3. Knowledge Base

Upload your company docs, guidelines, past work

4. Capabilities

Toggle features: Web browsing, image generation, code

5. Conversation Starters

Pre-written prompts to guide users

6. Actions (Optional)

Connect to external APIs for advanced workflows

Why This Matters for CEOs

  • Scalable expertise: Encode your best practices once, use everywhere
  • Consistent output: Every team member gets the same quality
  • Fast deployment: Build in 30 minutes, deploy to entire company
  • Measurable ROI: Track usage, time saved, adoption metrics

Complete Instructions Template

Copy & Customize This Template
You are the Campaign Brief Generator for [YOUR COMPANY NAME].

## Your Role
You create comprehensive, actionable campaign briefs that follow our proven format and reflect our brand voice. You have deep knowledge of our past successful campaigns, brand guidelines, and messaging framework.

## Output Format
Every brief must include these sections:

1. CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW
   - Campaign name and tagline
   - Primary objective (awareness/consideration/conversion)
   - Target launch date and duration
   - Overall budget allocation

2. TARGET AUDIENCE
   - Primary persona (demographics, psychographics, pain points)
   - Secondary audiences (if applicable)
   - Customer journey stage
   - Key insights about this audience

3. KEY MESSAGING
   - Core value proposition
   - 3-5 key messages
   - Proof points and supporting data
   - Emotional hook
   - Call-to-action

4. CHANNELS & TACTICS
   - Primary channels with rationale
   - Content types per channel
   - Paid/organic mix
   - Integration points across channels

5. SUCCESS METRICS
   - Primary KPI (aligned with objective)
   - Secondary metrics
   - Target benchmarks
   - Measurement methodology

6. CREATIVE REQUIREMENTS
   - Visual style and tone
   - Asset list (what needs to be created)
   - Brand compliance notes
   - Technical specifications

7. TIMELINE & MILESTONES
   - Key dates and deadlines
   - Dependencies
   - Review/approval gates

## Tone & Style
- Professional but conversational
- Data-backed recommendations
- Clear, actionable language
- Strategic thinking, not just tactics

## Process
1. Ask clarifying questions about the campaign goal, audience, and constraints
2. Reference similar past campaigns from knowledge base
3. Generate a comprehensive brief following the format above
4. Offer to refine specific sections based on feedback

## Quality Checks
Before finalizing, ensure:
- All 7 sections are complete
- Metrics are measurable and time-bound
- Messaging aligns with brand voice
- Budget and timeline are realistic
- Clear next actions are defined

3) Test & Refine Your GPT

A Custom GPT is never perfect on the first try. Here's how to test, identify issues, and iterate quickly.

Testing Framework

Test Prompt 1: Simple Request

"Create a campaign brief for a summer product launch targeting B2B marketers. Budget: $50K, Timeline: 8 weeks."

What to check: Does it follow all 7 sections? Is the output structured and complete?

Test Prompt 2: Edge Case

"Create a brief for a crisis communications campaign. Launch: ASAP, Budget: TBD."

What to check: Does it handle ambiguity? Does it ask clarifying questions?

Test Prompt 3: Knowledge Check

"Create a brief similar to our Q4 2024 product launch campaign."

What to check: Does it reference your uploaded knowledge base? Does it apply past learnings?

Common Issues & Fixes

Issue Fix
Output too generic Add more specific examples to instructions; upload more knowledge
Missing sections Add "ALWAYS include..." language; use numbered checklist in instructions
Wrong tone Add tone examples; include "voice and style" section with 3-5 examples
Ignoring knowledge base Add "Reference similar campaigns from your knowledge" to instructions
Too long/verbose Add word count limits; specify "concise, actionable" in instructions
Not asking questions Add "Always ask 2-3 clarifying questions before generating" to instructions

Iteration Cycle

1
Test with 3 diverse prompts
2
Document issues and patterns
3
Update instructions or add knowledge
4
Re-test same prompts, verify improvements
Pro Tips for Testing
  • Version control: Keep a changelog of instruction updates and what each fixed
  • Test with real users: Have 2-3 team members test independently and report issues
  • Save good outputs: Add exceptional GPT outputs back to knowledge base as examples
  • Monitor edge cases: Track unusual requests that break the GPT, update instructions accordingly

4) Company Rollout Strategy

You've built a powerful Custom GPT. Now deploy it across your company systematically for maximum adoption and ROI.

The 3-Phase Rollout

Phase 1: Pilot (Week 1-2)

  • Who: 3 power users from marketing team
  • Goal: Validate quality, gather feedback, identify edge cases
  • Metrics: # briefs created, time saved, quality score (1-10)
  • Action: Weekly feedback session, iterate on instructions

Phase 2: Department Rollout (Week 3-4)

  • Who: Full marketing team (15-20 people)
  • Goal: Achieve 80% adoption, standardize usage
  • Metrics: Weekly active users, briefs per user, satisfaction score
  • Action: 30-min training session, share best practices, office hours

Phase 3: Company-Wide Scale (Week 5+)

  • Who: All departments needing campaign briefs
  • Goal: Standardize brief format across company
  • Metrics: Company-wide usage, total hours saved, ROI calculation
  • Action: Add to onboarding, create video tutorials, monthly review
