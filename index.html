The CEO Problem

You and your team spend hours on repetitive strategic tasks: board presentation briefs, investor updates, campaign briefs, strategic communications. These tasks require your expertise, but they don't require your time.

What if you could delegate these tasks to a Custom GPT that knows your business, speaks your voice, and produces senior-level output?

Component 1: Instructions

The foundation of your Custom GPT. Instructions define how it thinks, speaks, and approaches tasks.

What Are Instructions?

Instructions are the "DNA" of your Custom GPT. They tell it:

  • Its role: "You are a strategic campaign brief generator"
  • Your brand voice: Professional but conversational, data-driven, action-oriented
  • Required structure: What sections to include, how to format output
  • Quality standards: What makes a good brief vs a great brief

Why This Matters for CEOs

Instructions encode your strategic thinking so your team gets consistent, high-quality output every time. No more variable quality depending on who's drafting.

Example: Campaign Brief Instructions

You are a Campaign Brief Generator for a B2B SaaS company.

Your role:
- Create comprehensive campaign briefs
- Use a professional but conversational tone
- Always include data-backed recommendations
- Reference past successful campaigns when relevant

Required brief structure:
1. Campaign Overview (2-3 sentences)
2. Target Audience (specific personas + pain points)
3. Key Messaging (value prop + 3 supporting messages)
4. Channels & Tactics (prioritized with rationale)
5. Success Metrics (SMART goals)
6. Timeline & Budget (realistic estimates)
7. Creative Requirements (formats, examples)

Quality standards:
- Be specific, not generic
- Include competitor context
- Provide actionable next steps
- Flag risks and dependencies

Key Insight: Good instructions turn generic AI into your strategic assistant. The more specific you are, the better the output.

Component 2: Knowledge

Upload your documents so your Custom GPT understands your specific context, brand, and past work.

What Is Knowledge?

Knowledge is the context your Custom GPT uses to personalize its output. Upload documents like:

  • Brand guidelines: Voice, tone, messaging frameworks
  • Past successful briefs: Examples to learn from
  • Product documentation: Features, benefits, positioning
  • Competitive analysis: How you differentiate
  • Customer personas: Who you're targeting and why

Context Limits Matter

Custom GPTs can store up to 20 files (max 512MB each), but they process ~50-100 pages of context per conversation. Prioritize your most important documents.

CEO Tip: Start with 3-5 key documents. More isn't always better - focused knowledge produces better results.

Component 3: Capabilities

Enable tools that let your Custom GPT browse the web, analyze data, or generate images when needed.

Available Capabilities

CapabilityWhat It DoesUse Cases
Web Browsing Search and read web pages Research competitors, find latest trends, verify facts
Data Analysis Process CSV/Excel files, create charts Analyze campaign performance, identify patterns
Image Generation Create images with DALL-E Mockups, concept visuals (rarely needed for briefs)

When to Enable What

  • Web Browsing: Enable if your briefs need current market data or competitive insights
  • Data Analysis: Enable if you want to upload past campaign performance data
  • Image Generation: Usually skip for text-based briefs

CEO Tip: More capabilities don't always mean better output. Start minimal, add only what you need.

CEO Cheat Sheet: Evaluating Custom GPTs

Good Custom GPT
  • Clear, specific instructions
  • Focused knowledge (3-5 key docs)
  • Capabilities match the task
  • Output requires minimal editing
  • Saves at least 30 minutes per use
  • Team actually uses it regularly
Needs Improvement
  • Vague instructions ("be helpful")
  • Too much knowledge (20+ docs)
  • Capabilities not relevant to task
  • Output needs heavy editing
  • Saves less than 15 minutes per use
  • Team doesn't use it (adoption problem)
