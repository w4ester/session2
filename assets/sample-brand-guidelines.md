## Brand Voice & Tone

### Our Voice (Always This Way):
- **Professional but Approachable:** We're experts, but we don't talk down to people
- **Data-Driven:** We back up claims with evidence and metrics
- **Action-Oriented:** We focus on outcomes and results
- **Clear & Concise:** We respect people's time - no fluff

### Tone Variations (Adjust by Audience):

**For Board/Investors:**
- Strategic and forward-looking
- Financial and metrics-focused
- Confident and decisive
- High-level with supporting details

**For Internal Teams:**
- Collaborative and inclusive
- Transparent about challenges
- Motivational and inspiring
- Practical and actionable

**For Customers:**
- Helpful and supportive
- Solution-focused
- Empathetic to pain points
- Educational without being condescending

## Writing Guidelines

### Do's:
✅ Start with the most important information (pyramid structure)
✅ Use bullets and short paragraphs for readability
✅ Include specific numbers and data when possible
✅ Use active voice ("We launched" not "The product was launched")
✅ Be specific about actions and outcomes
✅ Ask clarifying questions when needed

### Don'ts:
❌ Use jargon without explanation
❌ Write long paragraphs (max 3-4 sentences)
❌ Make claims without evidence
❌ Use passive voice excessively
❌ Assume the reader has context
❌ Use buzzwords that don't add meaning

## Formatting Standards

### Headers:
- Use clear, descriptive headers
- Maximum 3 levels deep
- Use title case for main headers

### Lists:
- Use bullets for unordered items
- Use numbers for sequential steps
- Keep list items parallel in structure

### Numbers:
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use numerals for metrics/data
- Use commas in numbers over 999 (e.g., 1,000)
- Round percentages to one decimal place unless precision matters

### Dates:
- Use Month DD, YYYY format (e.g., January 15, 2025)
- Quarter references: Q1 2025, Q2 2025, etc.

## Common Scenarios & Examples

### Scenario 1: Announcing Good News
**Bad Example:**
"We had a great quarter and things are looking up."

**Good Example:**
"Q4 revenue reached $5.2M (+35% YoY), driven by 12 new enterprise customers and 95% retention. This positions us ahead of our Series A goals."

### Scenario 2: Addressing a Challenge
**Bad Example:**
"We're facing some headwinds in the market."

**Good Example:**
"Customer acquisition costs increased 15% in Q4 due to iOS 14 privacy changes. We've implemented three countermeasures: [1] diversified to LinkedIn ads, [2] increased organic content, [3] enhanced referral program. Early results show 8% CAC improvement."

### Scenario 3: Making a Recommendation
**Bad Example:**
"We should probably expand to Europe."

**Good Example:**
"Recommend expanding to UK/Germany in Q2 2025. Market analysis shows $10M TAM, 40% of inbound leads from region, and 3 enterprise prospects ready to sign. Requires $200K investment, expected payback in 9 months."
