**Primary Logo:**
- Full color logo on white/light backgrounds

- White logo on dark backgrounds

**What NOT to do:**
- ‚ùå Don't rotate, skew, or distort the logo
- ‚ùå Don't change logo colors

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice Attributes

**We are:**
1. **Direct** - Get to the point quickly, no fluff
2. **Confident** - We know our product works, we've seen the results
3. **Empowering** - You're smart, we're here to help you succeed
4. **Data-driven** - We back claims with real numbers and evidence

**We are NOT:**
1. **Corporate or stiff** - No jargon walls or bureaucratic language
2. **Cute or clever** - No forced puns or unnecessary wordplay
3. **Salesy or pushy** - No aggressive CTAs or fear-based marketing
4. **Overly technical** - No unnecessary complexity or showing off

### Tone by Channel

**Website Homepage:**
- Bold and benefit-focused
- Action-oriented language
- Clear value proposition in first 3 seconds
- Example: "Stop drowning in marketing tasks. Automate the busy work. Focus on strategy."

**Email Marketing:**
- Friendly but respectful of time
- Scannable with clear sections
- One clear CTA per email
- Example subject: "You're spending 60% of your time on this (here's how to fix it)"

**Social Media (LinkedIn):**
- Professional but human
- Story-driven, not feature-driven
- Thought leadership, not just promotion
- Example: "Our customer Sarah saved 8 hours last week. Here's what she did with that time..."

**Blog/Content:**
- Educational and actionable
- Depth without complexity
- Tactical advice, not theory
- Example title: "How to Automate Your Email Campaigns in 30 Minutes (Step-by-Step)"

**Product UI:**
- Clear and jargon-free
- Action-oriented button labels
- Helpful error messages
- Example button: "Start Automating" (not "Submit" or "Continue")

**Customer Support:**
- Empathetic and solution-focused
- Patient and thorough
- Acknowledge frustration, then solve
- Example: "That's frustrating‚Äîlet's fix this. Here's what we'll do..."

## Writing Style Guidelines

### General Principles

**Use active voice:**
- ‚úÖ "Automate your campaigns"
- ‚ùå "Campaigns can be automated"

**Write for skimmers:**
- Break up long paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Use bullet points liberally
- Bold key takeaways
- Add descriptive subheadings

**Lead with benefits, not features:**
- ‚úÖ "Save 5 hours per week on campaign setup"
- ‚ùå "Advanced automation engine with scheduling capabilities"

**Use specific numbers:**
- ‚úÖ "3x better conversion rates"
- ‚ùå "Significantly improved performance"

**Avoid jargon and buzzwords:**
- ‚ùå "Leverage synergies" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Work together more effectively"
- ‚ùå "Best-in-class solution" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "92% of customers still use it after 6 months"
- ‚ùå "Revolutionary AI" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "AI that saves you 5 hours per week"

### Sentence Structure

**Keep sentences short:**
- Aim for 15-20 words per sentence
- Break complex ideas into multiple sentences
- One idea per sentence

**Vary sentence length:**
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones
- Create rhythm: short, medium, short, longer, short

**Use contractions naturally:**
- ‚úÖ "You're" not "You are"
- ‚úÖ "We'll" not "We will"
- ‚úÖ "Don't" not "Do not"

### Word Choice

**Prefer simple words:**
- "Use" not "utilize"
- "Help" not "facilitate"
- "Buy" not "purchase"
- "Show" not "demonstrate"

**Use power words sparingly:**
- Save them for moments that matter
- Don't overuse: Amazing, Revolutionary, Game-changing
- Better alternatives: Proven, Measurable, Practical

**Be inclusive:**
- Use "they" instead of "he/she"
- "Team members" not "guys"
- "People" not "users" (when referring to customers)

## Messaging Frameworks

### Value Proposition Formula

**Structure:** [Outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Differentiator]

**Example:**
"Marketing automation that delivers ROI in weeks, not months‚Äîso your team can focus on strategy, not execution."

**Components:**
- Outcome: ROI
- Timeframe: weeks, not months
- Differentiator: focus on strategy vs execution

### Feature ‚Üí Benefit Translation

**Pattern:** [Feature] ‚Üí [So that] ‚Üí [Benefit]

**Example:**
- Feature: "AI-powered campaign optimization"
- So that: "You don't have to manually A/B test and adjust"
- Benefit: "Save 5+ hours per week and get 3x better results"

**Usage in marketing:**
"Our AI optimizes campaigns automatically‚Äîso you don't waste time on manual A/B testing‚Äîfreeing up 5+ hours per week for strategic work."

### Call-to-Action Hierarchy

**Primary CTA (High Intent):**
- "Start Free Trial"
- "Book a Demo"
- "See How It Works"
- Bold, high contrast, prominent placement

**Secondary CTA (Low Intent):**
- "Learn More"
- "Watch Video"
- "Download Guide"
- Outlined button, less prominent

**Tertiary CTA (Alternative):**
- "View Pricing"
- "Read Case Studies"
- "Contact Sales"
- Text link, minimal styling

## Content Guidelines by Asset Type

### Landing Pages

**Hero Section:**
- Headline (7-10 words): State the main benefit
- Subheadline (15-25 words): Expand on how you deliver that benefit
- CTA: One primary action
- Visual: Product in use (not stock photo)

**Social Proof:**
- Customer logos (recognizable brands)
- Specific testimonial quotes with attribution
- Key metrics (e.g., "487 companies trust us")

**Features/Benefits:**
- 3-5 key benefits max
- Lead with benefit, follow with feature explanation
- Use icons for scannability

**Example:**

```
Hero Headline: "Automate Your Marketing. Reclaim Your Time."

Subheadline: "Marketing teams using MarketAI save 5+ hours per week by automating repetitive tasks‚Äîso they can focus on strategy and creativity."

CTA: "Start Free Trial ‚Üí"
```

### Email Campaigns

**Subject Lines:**
- 6-10 words (mobile-friendly)
- Lead with benefit or curiosity
- Use numbers when relevant
- A/B test consistently

**Examples:**
- ‚úÖ "You're wasting 60% of your time on this"
- ‚úÖ "3 ways to automate your campaigns today"
- ‚úÖ "Sarah saved 8 hours this week‚Äîhere's how"
- ‚ùå "MarketAI Newsletter - June Edition"
- ‚ùå "Check out our latest features"

**Email Body:**
- One topic per email
- Scannable structure (headers, bullets, bold)
- One clear CTA
- Personal sign-off

### Social Media Posts

**LinkedIn:**
- Start with a hook (first line is critical)
- Tell a story or share a lesson
- Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)
- Include line breaks for readability
- End with a question to drive engagement
- Limit hashtags (2-3 max)

**Example:**

```
The best advice I got as a marketing leader: "Stop trying to do everything."

Three months ago, our team was running 12 campaigns across 8 platforms. We were exhausted. Performance was declining.

So we cut it in half. Six campaigns. Four platforms.

The result? 2x better performance and a team that's actually energized.

Less isn't lazy. It's strategic.

What have you stopped doing that made you more effective?
```

### Blog Posts

**Structure:**
1. Hook (first 2 sentences grab attention)
2. Promise (what they'll learn/gain)
3. Context (why this matters)
4. Tactical steps (the how-to)
5. Summary (key takeaways)
6. CTA (one clear next step)

**Formatting:**
- H2 every 300-400 words
- Bullet lists for scannability
- Bold key phrases
- Include examples
- Add visuals (screenshots, charts)

**Title formulas:**
- "How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe]"
- "[Number] Ways to [Solve Problem]"
- "Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong (And What to Do Instead)"
- "The [Process] That Helped [Company/Person] [Achieve Result]"

## Image & Visual Guidelines

### Photography Style

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Real customers using the product
- ‚úÖ Diverse representation (age, race, gender)
- ‚úÖ Natural lighting and authentic moments
- ‚úÖ Clean, uncluttered backgrounds
- ‚úÖ Professional but not overly staged

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Generic stock photos (especially handshakes)
- ‚ùå Overly posed or artificial scenarios
- ‚ùå Low resolution or pixelated images
- ‚ùå Busy backgrounds that distract
- ‚ùå Images that perpetuate stereotypes

### Data Visualization

**Charts & Graphs:**
- Use brand colors (purple for primary data series)
- Clean, minimal design (remove unnecessary gridlines)
- Clear labels and legends
- Mobile-responsive (readable at small sizes)
- Accessible (don't rely on color alone)

**Icons:**
- Consistent style (all outlined or all filled)
- Simple and recognizable
- Brand color or neutral gray
- Accessible (include alt text)

### UI Screenshots

**Product Screenshots:**
- Show real features solving real problems
- Annotate key areas if needed
- Use actual customer data (anonymized)
- Keep up-to-date with current UI
- Add subtle shadows for depth

## Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Do's ‚úÖ

- Lead with customer benefits, not product features
- Use specific numbers and data to support claims
- Tell customer success stories
- Be transparent about limitations
- Acknowledge competitors respectfully
- Use contractions and conversational language
- Break up text with visuals and white space
- Focus on outcomes and ROI

### Don'ts ‚ùå

- Use vague superlatives ("best," "revolutionary," "game-changing")
- Overpromise or exaggerate capabilities
- Bash competitors
- Use corporate jargon or buzzwords
- Write long, dense paragraphs
- Make claims without evidence
- Use fear-based marketing tactics
- Ignore accessibility standards

## Accessibility Standards

### Text & Color

- Minimum contrast ratio: 4.5:1 (body text), 3:1 (large text/headings)
- Don't rely on color alone to convey information
- Text size: minimum 16px for body copy
- Line height: minimum 1.5 for body text
- Allow text resizing up to 200% without loss of functionality

### Images & Media

- Provide alt text for all images
- Include captions/transcripts for videos
- Don't autoplay videos with sound
- Provide text alternatives for charts and graphs

### Navigation & Interaction

- Keyboard accessible (tab navigation works)
- Clear focus indicators
- Descriptive link text ("Read the guide" not "Click here")
- Form labels clearly associated with inputs
