# Campaign Brief Template

This is our standard template for creating marketing campaign briefs. Use this structure for all campaigns.

---

# Campaign Brief: [Campaign Name]

**Campaign Owner:** [Name]
**Date Created:** [Date]
**Campaign Timeline:** [Start Date] - [End Date]
**Total Budget:** [Amount]

---

## 1. Executive Summary

[2-3 sentence overview of the campaign: goal, target audience, key differentiator]

**Example:**
> Q2 Product Launch campaign targeting mid-market B2B SaaS companies. Goal: Generate 500 MQLs and 50 demos in 8 weeks. Key differentiator: AI-powered automation that delivers ROI in <30 days, unlike enterprise platforms that take months.

### Secondary Objectives:

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary Audience:
- **Title:** [Job titles]
- **Company Size:** [Employee count / revenue]
- **Industry:** [Industries]
- **Location:** [Geographic regions]

**Example:**
- **Title:** VP Marketing, Director of Marketing Ops, Head of Growth
- **Company Size:** 50-500 employees, $5-50M revenue
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS, Tech Services
- **Location:** North America (USA, Canada)

### Psychographics:
- **Pain Points:** [Top 3-5 problems they face]
- **Motivations:** [What drives their decisions]
- **Goals:** [What they're trying to achieve]
- **Objections:** [Why they might not buy]

**Example:**
- **Pain Points:**
  - Spending 60% of time on manual campaign execution
  - Managing 10+ disconnected marketing tools
  - Struggling to prove marketing ROI
  - Team is overwhelmed and burning out
  - Can't scale marketing without hiring more people

- **Motivations:**
  - Career advancement (prove marketing impact)
  - Team efficiency (do more with same team)
  - Data-driven decision making
  - Competitive advantage

- **Goals:**
  - Automate repetitive tasks
  - Improve campaign performance
  - Scale marketing operations
  - Spend more time on strategy

- **Objections:**
  - "Too expensive for our budget"
  - "Implementation takes too long"
  - "Our team doesn't have technical skills"
  - "We just invested in [competitor]"

### Buying Behavior:
- **Research channels:** [Where they look for solutions]
- **Decision criteria:** [What matters most]
- **Buying process:** [Steps they take]
- **Decision timeframe:** [How long it takes]

**Example:**
- **Research channels:** Google, peer recommendations, industry publications (MarTech, CMO.com), LinkedIn
- **Decision criteria:** ROI, ease of implementation, team adoption, integrations, support quality
- **Buying process:** Research (1-2 weeks) → Demo (2-4 tools) → Trial (2-4 weeks) → Purchase
- **Decision timeframe:** 4-8 weeks from awareness to purchase

---

## 4. Key Messaging

### Value Proposition:
[One sentence: Why us, why now]

**Example:**
> Marketing automation that delivers measurable ROI in weeks, not months—so your team can focus on strategy, not execution.

### Supporting Proof Points:
1. [Proof point 1 + evidence]
2. [Proof point 2 + evidence]
3. [Proof point 3 + evidence]

**Example:**
1. **Save 5+ hours per week** - Our customers report freeing up 25% of their time from manual tasks
2. **3x better performance** - Campaigns automated with our platform see 3x higher conversion rates
3. **Setup in hours, not months** - Average time to first campaign: 4 hours (vs 3+ months for enterprise platforms)

### Competitive Differentiation:
[What makes us different from alternatives]

**Example:**
Unlike enterprise platforms (Marketo, HubSpot) that require dedicated admins and 6-figure budgets, we deliver enterprise-grade automation for growing teams. And unlike simple tools (Mailchimp), we handle sophisticated multi-channel campaigns.

### Calls-to-Action:
- **Primary CTA:** [Main action]
- **Secondary CTA:** [Alternative action]

**Example:**
- **Primary CTA:** "Book a Demo"
- **Secondary CTA:** "Download the ROI Calculator"

## 5. Channel Strategy

### Channel Mix & Rationale:

| Channel | Budget | Rationale | Expected Results |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------------|
| [Channel 1] | $[Amount] | [Why] | [Expected outcome] |
| [Channel 2] | $[Amount] | [Why] | [Expected outcome] |
| [Channel 3] | $[Amount] | [Why] | [Expected outcome] |

**Example:**
| Channel | Budget | Rationale | Expected Results |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------------|
| LinkedIn Ads | $15,000 | Target audience is active, precise targeting | 250 MQLs @ $60 CPL |
| Email (Existing DB) | $2,000 | Warm audience, low cost | 150 MQLs @ $13 CPL |
| Content Marketing | $8,000 | SEO + thought leadership | 100 MQLs @ $80 CPL |
| Webinar | $5,000 | High-intent, builds trust | 50 demos, 200 attendees |

### Channel-Specific Tactics:

**LinkedIn Ads:**
- Sponsored content targeting job titles + company size
- Retargeting website visitors
- Lookalike audiences from customer list

**Email:**
- 3-email nurture sequence to existing leads
- Re-engagement campaign for cold leads
- Customer expansion to existing users

**Content Marketing:**
- 2 long-form blog posts (SEO-optimized)
- 1 comparison guide (us vs competitors)
- 5 LinkedIn posts from leadership

**Webinar:**
- "How to Automate Your Marketing in 30 Days" live event
- Panel with 2 customers sharing results
- Q&A + live demo

## 7. Timeline & Milestones

### Campaign Phases:

**Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-2):**
- Week 1: Finalize messaging, brief creative team
- Week 2: Build landing pages, set up tracking, QA

**Launch (Week 3):**
- Day 1: Email blast to existing database
- Day 2: LinkedIn ads go live
- Day 3: Content published, social promotion begins

**Active Campaign (Weeks 3-8):**
- Ongoing: Optimize based on performance data
- Week 5: Webinar event
- Week 6: Mid-campaign review, adjust budgets
- Week 8: Final push, increase spend on top performers

**Post-Campaign (Week 9-10):**
- Week 9: Lead scoring, handoff to sales
- Week 10: Performance analysis, retrospective

### Key Milestones:

| Date | Milestone | Owner |
|------|-----------|-------|
| [Date] | [Milestone] | [Name] |

**Example:**
| Date | Milestone | Owner |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Feb 15 | All assets complete | Creative team |
| Feb 20 | Campaign setup complete, QA done | Marketing Ops |
| Feb 25 | Campaign launch | Campaign Manager |
| Mar 18 | Webinar event | Demand Gen |
| Apr 1 | Lead handoff to sales | Sales Ops |
| Apr 8 | Performance report complete | Analytics |
