# Strategic Messaging Framework

This document outlines our key strategic themes, positioning, and messaging priorities. Reference these when drafting executive communications.

---

## Company Positioning

**One-liner:**
We help B2B companies automate their marketing operations so teams can focus on strategy, not execution.

**Expanded (2-3 sentences):**
Marketing teams waste 60% of their time on repetitive tasks—campaign setup, reporting, data cleaning. Our platform automates these workflows using AI, freeing marketers to focus on creative strategy and customer insights. The result: 10x faster campaign execution and 3x better performance.

**Target Customer:**
B2B companies with 50-500 employees, marketing teams of 3-10 people, running multi-channel campaigns across email, paid ads, and social. They're drowning in tools and manual work.

## Strategic Themes (Use These Consistently)

### Theme 1: From Execution to Strategy
**Key Message:** Marketing automation should free you to think strategically, not just execute faster.

**Supporting Points:**
- Most automation tools just speed up manual tasks
- We eliminate tasks entirely so you can focus on high-value work
- Our customers report 5+ hours/week gained for strategic planning

**Use When:**
- Differentiating from competitors
- Explaining our value prop to marketers
- Discussing product vision

**Example Usage:**
> "Our goal isn't to help you do busy work faster—it's to eliminate busy work entirely so you can focus on what matters: understanding your customers and crafting winning strategies."

---

### Theme 2: AI That Actually Works
**Key Message:** Most AI tools overpromise and underdeliver. We built AI that solves real problems with real ROI.

**Supporting Points:**
- We don't use "AI" as a buzzword—it's solving specific pain points
- Our customers see ROI in weeks, not months
- 92% of customers still using AI features after 6 months

**Use When:**
- Industry discussions about AI hype vs reality
- Differentiating our "practical AI" approach
- Addressing skepticism about AI

**Example Usage:**
> "We're done with AI hype. Our approach is simple: identify your biggest time sinks, automate them with AI, measure the results. No magic, just ROI."

---

### Theme 3: Enterprise-Grade for Growing Companies
**Key Message:** You shouldn't have to choose between power and simplicity. We deliver both.

**Supporting Points:**
- Built for teams that are scaling but not yet Fortune 500
- Enterprise features without enterprise complexity or price
- Grows with you from 5 to 50 person marketing team

**Use When:**
- Positioning against enterprise tools (Marketo, HubSpot)
- Positioning against simple tools (Mailchimp, ConvertKit)
- Explaining our market positioning

**Example Usage:**
> "We built for the companies in the middle—too sophisticated for simple tools, too nimble for enterprise bloatware. You get power without complexity."

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Enterprise Platforms (Marketo, HubSpot, Pardot)
**They say:** "We're the complete solution"
**We say:** "They're built for Fortune 500s with dedicated admins and six-figure budgets. We're built for fast-growing companies that need power without complexity."

**Key differentiators:**
- Setup in hours, not months
- 10x lower price point
- AI-native (not bolted on)

---

### vs. Point Solutions (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign)
**They say:** "We're easy to use"
**We say:** "They're great for simple email campaigns. We're for sophisticated multi-channel marketing operations."

**Key differentiators:**
- Cross-channel orchestration (not just email)
- AI automation (not just templates)
- Built for teams, not solopreneurs

---

### vs. Build-Your-Own (Zapier + Multiple Tools)
**They say:** "Build your own stack"
**We say:** "That's 10+ tools to integrate and maintain. We're the unified platform that just works."

**Key differentiators:**
- Single platform (vs. 10+ tools)
- Native integrations (vs. fragile Zaps)
- Support and reliability

**Focus on:** ROI, team productivity, competitive advantage

### For Marketing Leaders:

### For Marketing Operators:
**Focus on:** Ease of use, technical capabilities, integrations
**Example:** "Native integrations with your entire stack. Setup in hours. AI that actually works."

## What to Avoid

**❌ Generic AI hype**
- Don't: "AI-powered revolutionary platform"
- Do: "AI that eliminates 5 hours of manual work per week"

**❌ Feature lists without benefits**
- Don't: "We have 47 features including X, Y, Z..."
- Do: "Three capabilities that matter: [benefit], [benefit], [benefit]"

**❌ Vague claims**
- Don't: "Best-in-class solution"
- Do: "92% of customers still actively use our AI features after 6 months"

**❌ Discounting competitors**
- Don't: "HubSpot is terrible"
- Do: "HubSpot is great for enterprises with dedicated admins. We're built for growing teams."

## Brand Voice

**We are:**
- Confident (but not arrogant)
- Direct (but not blunt)
- Smart (but not academic)
- Human (but professional)

**We are not:**
- Cute or clever
- Corporate or stiff
- Technical or jargon-heavy
- Salesy or pushy

**Tone by context:**
- **Marketing website:** Bold, confident, benefit-focused
- **Product:** Clear, helpful, jargon-free
- **Support:** Empathetic, patient, solution-oriented
- **Executive comms:** Transparent, strategic, authentic
